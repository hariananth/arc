Main Narrative

A wave of uprisings rocked the Middle East following what the international community dubbed the “Arab Spring” of 2010. These protests centered around civilians dissatisfaction with corrupt leadership and militaristic regimes, and were further fueled by existing religious and ethnic divides.  

While most of these protests began as non-violent demonstrations, many of them elicited violent responses from government forces — some even resulting in civil war. Such was the case in Libya under the rule of Gaddafi and any form of opposition to the regime was handled swiftly by the military.

Foley

In April 2011, Foley and three other Western journalists were ambushed by Gaddafi's forces in Libya, resulting in the death of one and the rest taken prisoner.

"A soldier's pressing your face into the bed of a truck, bleeding from the scalp — it's the worst kind of shock," Foley said afterwards.

For 18 days, it was unknown whether he was still alive.

Foley’s parents, Diane and John Foley, campaigned for his release, holding prayer vigils and working with US and Arab diplomatic teams to gather information.

His experience of being captured however did not deter him.

"It doesn't always repel you. Sometimes… it draws you closer. Feeling like you've survived something — it's a strange sort of force that you are drawn back to," he said.

ISIS originated as a splinter group from Iraq-based al Qaeda. It aims to create an Islamic state governed by Sharia law across Sunni areas of Iraq and Syria. ISIS is said to have been established in 2006, but it did not begin gaining significant advances in territory until 2013. In June of 2014 they quickly swept through northern Iraq and gained control of major cities Mosul, Tikrit, and the northeast corner of Syria.

Foley

Foley was kidnapped for a second time in Syria in November 2012 along with his translator and British journalist John Cantile. The three men were working on a film profiling the Free Syrian Army, a rebel group that opposed Assad’s regime and radical Islamic groups such as ISIS.  Foley was believed to be captured by a militant group loyal to President Assad.

Foley’s captors demanded incredibly high ransoms from his family, his employers and the United States government. Foley’s family even went so far as to consider breaking US law and paying a ransom to ISIS after receiving threatening emails from ISIS soldiers.

In June 2014 a fellow hostage was released and recounted a memorized message from Foley to his family in which Foley described his captivity in a cell with several other hostages. This became known as his final letter and his family later posted it on their Facebook page shortly after his death.

Syrian Civil War

The Syrian Civil War is an ongoing armed conflict taking place in Syria. The unrest began in the early spring of 2011 with nationwide protests against President Bashar al-Assad's government, whose forces responded with violent crackdowns. The conflict gradually morphed from popular protests to an armed rebellion after months of military sieges. The armed opposition consists of various groups that were formed during the course of the conflict, primarily the Islamic Front and the Free Syrian Army.

In 2013, Hezbollah entered the war in support of the Syrian army. In the east, the Islamic State (IS), a jihadist militant group originating from the Iraq War, made rapid military gains in both Syria and Iraq, eventually conflicting with the other rebels. In July 2014, ISIS controlled a third of Syria's territory and most of its oil and gas production, thus establishing itself as the major opposition force.

The Syrian government is upheld by military support from Russia, which was expanded in the winter of 2013–14, and Iran, while Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Turkey and United States transferred weapons to the rebels. The US, the UK and European and Arab states have declared a readiness to support the mainstream Syrian opposition. Owing to the nature of this involvement, the international response to the conflict has been described as a proxy war.
